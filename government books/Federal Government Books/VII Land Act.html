VII Land Act

Proposed: May 1, 2020
Singed: May 25, 2020



Section 1: Declaration of Land

As stated in the Declaration of Elstreet, The Union of Elstreet declares that all land disproved and undiscovered shall be claimed to Elstreet, and that any formation of a new Nation without approval from the House, shall be viewed as a declaration of war against the security of the Union.

Section 2: Types of Land

There shall be 2 types of Land, Federal Land and State Land.
        Federal Land shall be owned and controlled by the Executive and Legislative branch of the Government.

Section 3: Departments of Federal Land Managemen

Federal Lands may be divided into multiple districts and run by 5 Government departments, the Department of Defense, The National Park Service, The Fish and Wildlife Service, The Forest Service, and the Bureau of Land Management. Each department may have the goal of conversation, recreation, and extraction.
        The Forest Service may focus on the extraction and conversation of Forests and Grasslands, The National Park Service may focus on the conversation recreation of National Parks, The Fish and Wildlife Service may solely focus on the conversation of plant wildlife, and the Bureau of Land Management which shall focus on all subject of land management including leasing land to private citizens and business.

Section 4: Representation

As stated in the Constitution, the House may have total control of Federal Land. People living in Federal land will then be represented by the House, and People living in Federal Lands may follow the district's laws and regulation.

Section 5: Statehood

They're shall two ways for a Federal Territory to become a State.
        The First is that a voting member of the House may propose a new State. This proposed State must have its borders completely established, and must contain either a railroad or Trail inside its borders.
        If there happened to be people living in the proposed State then it must go through the second process which is that over a majority of people living in the territory must first approve of Statehood, then with its boards establish it may be voted upon in the House.
        Session of the Union will be viewed as an act of war and people associated with session will be permanently banned.

Section 6: Counties

State Governments may have the ability to create and establish their own Counties with the approval of the people in the county.
        Counties shall enforce State laws on a more local level, and are there to represent a part of a State. A County may provide a police force, jails, build roads and utilities, enforce taxes, manage schools, provide emergency management, Land zoning, and other things for the local citizens with the help of the State.
        Counties may have elected officials running the County and are voted by the people in that County.
        Counties must obey State and Federal laws and can not go against the laws written, but may make local laws as long they don't go against.

Section 7: State Powers

States may have the power to pass laws that may protect and or preserve their natural resources by either relegating mining or making it illegal to mine in certain places. Owners of Private Property may have the right to all resources in their land unless the land was already protected before the purchase, which the owner will then be required to follow said laws in place unless told otherwise.
        States may have the ability to create fixed properties for sale, and establish a price and create their rules of requirement for ownership as long as they don't go against the Federal Government.
