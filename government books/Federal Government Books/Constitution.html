The Constitution

Proposed: May 1, 2020
Singed: May 25, 2020

In order to form a more perfect union, establish justice, insure peace, have common defense, promote the general welfare, and to protect our rights and liberties. It is important that we the people establish the Constitution of the Union of Elstreet.



Article 1: The Legislative

Section 1

All legislative powers herein granted shall be vested in The House of the Elstreet Union. The House may be composed of the Hots, CO Hosts, Speaker, Temporary Speaker, and the Mayors.
        Voting Members may only include the Mayors with the speaker only able to vote as a tiebreaker. A term of the House may last for 4 months starting on January 1st and will be on the same schedule as the election dates of Mayors.

Section 2

The Host shall be the owner and Creator of the Elstreet World. Together the Host and CO Hots shall have the most power in Elstreet Government, with the power overrule anyone or anything, and establish new laws and documentation as they seem fit.
        The CO Host may only be picked by the Main Host,The CO Host shall have the same powers as the Main Host with the exception of the overruling of any Main Host decisions. There can only be 2 CO Host, and the Main Host may remove or add someone as CO host as they seem fit.
        The goal of the Host and CO Host is to make sure that the government as a whole, is running as efficiently as possible, and to protect national security.

Section 3

The Speaker of the House shall be the Vice President. If the Vice President is unable to fulfill their duty as Speaker then all voting members of the House will then vote for a Temporary Speaker. This Temporary Speaker will perform almost all the duties as Speaker including tie breaker.
        The Speaker may be chosen aligned with the Presidential Election.

Section 4

The House shall be composed of all Mayors in the several States, and chosen every 4 months by the People of the several States.
        In order for a State to elect a Mayor, the state must first have a population of 2 to more. To become Mayor of a State, The person must be a citizen of Elstreet, and must be inhabitant of that State for at least 4 months.
        Once a Mayor is elected, it shall be their duty to faithfully represent their State, and to govern their State as Mayor. There shall not be any term limits put into the position of Mayor.
        When vacancies happen in the House from any State, the Executive Authority shall issue a Special Election to fill such Vacancies.
        Mayors of the House shall as the desire of one fifth of those present. request a vote in the House.
        The House may vote to a separate document on the times of the Elections for the Mayors, President, and Speaker. This Vote shall require the 80% rule. The House shall meet at least once a year on the 2nd Monday in January.

Section 5

The House may determine the rules of its Proceedings, punishing members for disorderly behavior, and with 80% vote from the Mayors, expel a member.
        The House shall keep a journal of its proceedings, and from time to time publish this document that shall contain the Yeas and Nays of any vote.

Section 6

Members of the House shall receive a Compensation of their service, to be by law, and paid out of the Federal Bank.
        Members of the House shall in all cases, except Treason, Felony, and Breach of the Peace, be dismissed of arrest during their attendance in a session of the House.
        All bills for raising revenue and the creation of tax must be voted on by The House and require 80% approval among the House.

Section 7

The House shall have the power to lay and collect taxes, Duties, Imposts, and Excises, in order to pay the Debt and provide for the common defense and general welfare of the Union of Elstreet. But all Duties and Imposts and Excises shall be uniform throughout the Union.
        The House may have the power to coin money, and regulate the value thereof, and fix the standard of weights and measurement. Have the Power to borrow money on the credit of the Union of Elstreet. Have the Power to regulate Commerce with foreign nations, and among the several states and tritons owned by the Government. Have the power to make uniform laws on the subject of Bankruptcies throughout the Union of Elstreet. And have the power to provide punishment of counterfeiting the Securities and current money of the Union of Elstree.
        The House may have the power to establish post offices, roads and railroads across the Union of Elstreet.
        The House shall have the power to declare war, grant letters of Marque and Reprisal, and make rules concerning capture on land and water. Have the power to raise and support Armies. Have the power to make rules and regulations of all armed forces. To provide for the calling forth of the militia to execute the laws of the Union, suppress insurrections and repel invasions.
        The House shall have the power to have a federal district that is controlled solely by the House and is where the Government is seated. The House may also, with the permission of the state, take control over land and buildings for the purpose of Forts, Ports, Arsenals, and other needful usage.
        The House may have the power to establish a uniform rule of Naturalization and Citizenship with an 80% vote of all voting members.

Section 8

No members of The Legislative , Executive, and Judicial Branch during the time of their position, be allowed to hold any other Office in the Government.



Article 2: The Executive

Section 1

The powers of the executive branch shall be vested in a President of The Union of Elstreet. The President shall hold office during a term of 6 months, and together with the Vice President, chosen for the same term, be elected as follows.
        The President and Vice President must first have lived and been a citizen of Elstreet for at least 3 months in order to qualify. Then a vote is done by the Mayors of the House to determine who is President. If a President can not be decided by the Mayors then the Hosts will decide.
        The House must vote on a uniform date of when the President and Vice President is voted. The Vice President may be chosen by the President as the running mate during the Presidential Election.

Section 2

The President must faithfully represent and execute the law, and shall command the officers of the Union of Elstreet.
        The President shall be the commander and chief of the national army and the militias of the several states, when called into actual service of the Union of Elstreet.
        The President may create a cabinet of advisers, with approval of the Mayors, to run executive departments, and give the President important information and opinion on subject matters.
        The President can sign treaties but must then be approved by the House with an 80% vote of the current seated House. The President may also have the ability to grant reprieves and pardons for law breaking offensives except in case of Impeachment.

Section 3

The President may with the approval of the House, to appoint Ambassadors, other Ministers and Consuls, Judges to the Supreme Courts, and other appointees not provided in this Constitution but is mentioned by law.

Section 4

The President shall from time to time give to the House information of the State of the Union, and recommend considerations and measures that the President shall find fit.The President may call a special session meeting of the House.
        In the case of disagreement between the House on the next session meeting, the President may then decide on the next meeting.
        When a bill is passed by the House, it is then either signed into law by the President or is vetoed. A vetoed bill can be overruled with an 80% vote from all the Mayors.
        The President and Vice President may receive for their services a compensation, that shall neither be increased or decreased during their term in office. And shall not receive any other Emolument from the Union of Elstreet or from any State.

Section 5

The Vice President shall take the role as speaker of the House, and will be used as a tiebreaker in the House among the Mayors.
        In the case of removal of the President from office, or of the President's resignation, or inability to perform the powers of said office. Then the Vice President may be second in line and be given powers of the President. If the Vice President is also not able to fulfill the role as President then the Host and CO Host shall take charge until the next Presidential Election.

Section 6

The President, Vice President, Mayors and all Officers of the Union Of Elstreet, shall be removed from office on Impeachment for, and conviction of, Treason, Bribery, or other high crimes and misdemeanors.



Article 3: The Judicial

Section 1

The Judicial power of the Union of Elstreet, shall be vested in one Supreme Court, and in such infer courts as the House may for time to time create and establish. The Judges, both of the Supreme Court and inferior courts, shall hold their office for life as long in good behavior, and shall at times receive for their service a compensation, which shall not be diminished during their time in office.

Section 2

The Judicial power shall extend to all cases, in Law and equity, arise under the Constitution, The Laws of the Union of Elstreet and its several States, and treaties that have been made. The Supreme Court may have a Judicial Review on any law passed in the House, and can decide whether the law being put on trial is constitutional or not.

Section 3

The Federal Judicial power shall extend all cases affecting, Ambassadors, other public Ministers and Counsels. Extend to all cases of admiralty and maritime jurisdiction. Extend to all controversies to which the Union of Elstreet, and shall be a party to controversies between two or more States. Extend to cases between citizens and different States. Extend to cases between citizens of the same State claiming land under grants of different States. Extend to cases between a State, or the citizens thereof, and a foreign State, citizens or subjects.

Section 4

In all cases affecting Ambassadors, other public Ministers, and Counsels, and those in which a State shall be a party, the Supreme Court shall have jurisdiction of these cases. In all other cases mentioned before, the Supreme Court shall have appellate jurisdiction, unless told otherwise by law, made in the House with an 80% vote.

Section 5

The trial of all crimes except in case of impeachment, shall be jury, and such trial shall be held in the State where the said crime has been committed, but when not committed within any State, the trial shall be at such place or places as the House may direct.

Section 6

The Supreme Court shall be made up of up to 7 Judges, that are appointed by the President and approved by a vote in the House. When adding a new Judge to the Supreme Court, the total number of Judges shall not be an even number, so that a decision made by the Supreme Court shall not be a tie.
        The House may determine the rules on how courts are run.
        Judgement in case of Impeachment shall not extend further than to remove from Office, and disqualification to hold and enjoy any office of honor. Once impeached they are then subject to any further Trials and punishment according to law.
        The House may have the power to create tribunals inferior to the Supreme Court.



Article 4: Full Faith And Credits

Section 1

Credits and judicial proceedings given in each State shall be upheld in all other states. Citizens of each state shall be entitled to all privileges and immunities of citizens in other States.
        If a person is charged in any state with treason, felony, or other crimes and has fled to another state, shall be demanded by the executive authority, go back to the state where the crime was committed.

Section 2

New States may be admitted to the House into this Union. But no new state shall be formed or erected within the jurisdiction of any other, nor shall any State be formed by the junction of two or more States, or parts of States, without the approval of the Legislature of the States and the approval of the House.
        The House shall have the power to make rules and regulations respecting the Territory or other property belonging to the Union of Elstreet, and how a Territory can become a State.

Section 3

The Union of Elstreet shall guarantee every State a republican form of government, and shall be protected against invasion, and if the House approves then they shall also be protected against domestic violence.



Article 5: Amendments

Section 1

The House, whenever over 80% of all voting members shall deem it necessary, shall propose Amendments to this Consecution, or if 80% of legislators among the several States shall call in a convention to make Amendments and Changes to this Constitution.
        The ratification of such additions or changes, will then require 80% approval of the House and over 50% approval from legislators among the several States.
        Once an amendment is passed or anything that has already been written to This Constitution or any amendments shall never be removed but can only be counter unless there is a Constitutional convention in session.



Article 6: Powers of this Constitution

Section 1

All debts contracted and all engagement enter into, before the adoption of this Constitution, shall still be valid under this new Constitution.

Section 2

This constitution and the laws and treaties made under the Union of Elstreet, shall be the supreme law of the land. And the judges in every State Shall be bound to all things in the Constitution and or laws of any State to the contrary notwithstanding.
        All members of the House, State legislator, Supreme court, and other officers of the Union of Elstreet in all states, shall be bound by an Oath or affirmation to support this constitution, but no religious test shall ever be required as a qualification to any office or public trust under the Union of Elstreet.



Article 7: Ratifying the Constitution

Section 1

The Ratification of this Constitution shall require 80% of the States to approve.
