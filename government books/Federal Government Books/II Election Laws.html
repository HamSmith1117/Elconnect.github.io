II Election Laws

Proposed: May 1, 2020
Singed: May 25, 2020
Revised: July 25, 2020



Section 1: Election Dates

For the office of President of the Union of Elstreet, the election shall take place one week for January 1st and July 1st of every year. If there are any complications during the election, then the House has until either January 1st or July 1st to make the results final. Once someone is elected President, the person has until the week after January 1st or July 1st to be sworn in as president.
        If the person elected President decides to drop out of office or is not sworn in on time, then the Host and CO Host shall take charge until the next Presidential Election.
        For the office of Mayor of a State, the election shall take place one week before January 1st, May 1st, and September 1st, of every year. If there are any complications during the election, then the State has until either January 1st, May 1st, or September 1st to make a final result. Once someone is elected Mayor, the person has until the week after January 1st, May 1st, or September 1st to be sworn in as Mayor.
        If the person elected Mayor decides to drop out of office or is not sworn in on time, then a special election for a new Mayor must be held.

Section 2: Ballot Dates

In a Presidential Election, in order to be on the ballot you must have started your camping at least 2 months before the election date.
        In a Mayor Election, in order to be on the ballot you must have started your camping at least 1 months before the election date.

Section 3: Federal Voting Laws

To vote in any Federal or State elections, a person must be a citizen of Elstreet, and must present their ID when voting. A person can only vote in the State and or Jurisdiction that is listed on their ID.
        Any person who votes more than once in the election held in both the State and Federal level, or deposits more than one ballot for the same office as his or her vote at such election, or knowingly attempts to vote when not entitled to do so, shall be guilty under fraudulent voting.
        States may have control on where the voting both will be and how the ballot will look when voting, but all ballots must contain all official candidates running in their respective election.
        A person may only run for one election at a time.
        If an incumbent of one office fails to win a different office while their office was also up for election, then they will lose both offices, since they can only run for one office at a time.
