I House Rules

Proposed: May 1, 2020
Singed: May 25, 2020



Section 1: Members of The Hous

Members of the House may include but is not limited to, the Mayors, President and Vice President, Speaker and Temporary Speaker of the House, the Host, and CO Host.
        A term of the House may last for 4 months starting on January 1st and will be on the same schedule as the election dates of Mayors.

Section 2: Speaker of The House

The goal of the Speaker is to keep the House in order and the members decorum. As Speaker any political affiliation to a party should be denounced for the remainder of their term until running in election. Decision making shall be done in the most unbiased and most informed manner.
        A meeting may be called by the Speaker of the House. At the end of each meeting the next meeting should be discussed and scheduled. The Speaker may call for suspension of the House if the members have become unruly or in the case of emergency.
        The Speaker may also suspend a member of the House for the remainder of the meetings if they are acting unruly and are not cooperating with the Speaker's multiple requests.

Section 3: Writing a Bills

Any Bill proposed to the House must be written in the following format.
        A Heading counting the title or the bill number that it has been assigned. Underneath if not yet labeled in the title, will be the bill number. Every bill shall be numbered in order, to keep track of bills. Underneath that will be the dates of when the bill was proposed to the House, and if signed into law, then the date of signage shall be added.
        When writing a bill it shall be required to divide the bill into multiple Sections or Articles, depending on the length and subject matter of the bill.
        Within a Section, each clause, statement or paragraph, may be paragraphed and indented, to keep organization.

Section 4: Proposing and passing Bills

A bill may be drafted by anyone but only members of the House can propose the bill to the Speaker. The Speaker is obligated to decide on what bill is brought to the floor, if a bill is not put to the floor within 1 months then the bill is terminated. The Speaker must pick a bill to propose no matter what their political stands are.
        Once the bill is brought to the floor it may go through 3 forms of debates that each may be limited to only 1 day. An extra day to debate may be given if 80% of the current seating House votes for an extra day, but they may only have 3 extra days to debate. Once the bill has been debated it will then go to a final vote.
        Debates should be scheduled ahead of time by the Speaker. Members that may want to speak in the debate must tell the Speaker in advance so then the Speaker may give equal and appropriate time for those members to speak.
        When voting, all voting members must be counted in the vote, but if someone is not present in the vote then they will be counted as an abstain.
        Once the bill is passed it will then go to the President where they will either sign the bill into law or veto the bill. Once the bill is signed into law it is then properly documented and promulgated.

Section 5: Changes to this bill

Any changes to this bill shall require the approval of 80% of all voting members of the house, plus an extra 6 debates should be done before the vote.
